About Cyberbullying

What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital communication tools to deliberately and repeatedly harass, threaten, or humiliate someone. It can occur through social media, text messages, emails, online forums, and gaming platforms.

Impact on Students

Cyberbullying can lead to anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, academic problems, and in severe cases, self-harm. Victims often feel isolated and powerless, with the harassment following them everywhere through their devices.

Presentation Capsule

Session Summary

Our comprehensive cyberbullying awareness program covered essential topics designed to empower students with knowledge and practical skills for navigating the digital world safely.

Understanding Cyberbullying

Defined cyberbullying, identified different forms, and discussed the psychological impact on victims.

Digital Footprint

Taught students about the permanence of online actions and how to manage their digital presence responsibly.

Prevention Strategies

Provided practical tips for preventing cyberbullying and protecting personal information online.

Bystander Intervention

Empowered students to become upstanders and safely intervene when witnessing cyberbullying.

Reporting Procedures

Detailed step-by-step guidance on how to report cyberbullying incidents to appropriate authorities.

Support Resources

Provided comprehensive list of helplines, counseling services, and online safety resources.
